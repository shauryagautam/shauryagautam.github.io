User Research
Dec 2024

Talk to Users Before Writing Code

The biggest mistake PMs make is building solutions before understanding the problem. Spend 80% of your time in discovery. Interview 20+ users, observe their workflows, and identify patterns. Only then should you think about features.

Metrics
Dec 2024

Choose One North Star Metric

Too many metrics create confusion. Define one North Star that represents real value to users and business. For a social app, it's not signups—it's weekly active connections. For SaaS, it's not trials—it's activated users who hit their "aha moment."

Execution
Dec 2024

Ship Fast, Learn Faster

Perfect is the enemy of shipped. Launch MVPs in weeks, not months. Get real user feedback early. A feature used by 100 real users teaches you more than 1000 internal debates. Iterate based on data, not opinions.

MVP Agile Iteration
Prioritization
Dec 2024

Master the Art of Saying No

Every "yes" to a feature is a "no" to something else. Use RICE framework: Reach × Impact × Confidence / Effort. Ruthlessly cut features that don't move your North Star. Your roadmap should be a list of "not nows," not just "yeses."

Collaboration
Dec 2024

Engineering is Your Partner, Not Your Team

Great PMs don't "manage" engineers—they collaborate. Involve them in discovery, respect technical constraints, and trust their expertise. Write clear PRDs, but be open to better solutions. The best features come from PM-Eng brainstorming.

Innovation
Dec 2024

Solve Problems, Not Build Features

Users don't want features—they want problems solved. Instead of "Add export to PDF," think "Help users share reports with stakeholders." This mindset opens up multiple solutions and often leads to simpler, better products.

Essential PM Frameworks

RICE Prioritization

Reach × Impact × Confidence / Effort. Score features objectively to build a data-driven roadmap.

Jobs-to-be-Done

Understand what "job" users hire your product for. Focus on outcomes, not features.

AARRR Metrics

Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue, Referral. Track the full user lifecycle.

Kano Model

Classify features as Basic, Performance, or Delighters to prioritize what truly matters.

Opportunity Solution Tree

Map opportunities to outcomes, then explore multiple solutions before committing.

North Star Framework

One metric that captures core value. Align entire team around this single goal.

PM Mental Models

First Principles Thinking

Break problems down to fundamental truths. Question every assumption. Instead of "competitors do X," ask "what's the best way to solve this from scratch?"

Second-Order Thinking

Consider consequences of consequences. "If we add this feature, users will do X, which leads to Y, which might cause Z." Think 3 steps ahead.

Inversion

Instead of "how do we succeed?", ask "how could we fail?" Identify and eliminate failure modes. Often easier than finding success paths.
